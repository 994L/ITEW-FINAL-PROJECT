01. Marcos Twin Mansion Tour

Cabuyao, Laguna, houses the Marcos Twin Mansion, a structure steeped in history and debate. Built during Ferdinand Marcos's rule in the 80s, it stands as a symbol of a tumultuous era. Its purpose remains unclear. Some say it was for the Marcos twins, while official records claim it was for government use. Regardless, the mansion became synonymous with alleged extravagance during the Marcos regime. Sequestered by the government after the 1986 People Power Revolution, it's now under the Presidential Commission on Good Government. The future of the Marcos Twin Mansion is undecided, with proposals ranging from museums to demolition. Preserved or repurposed, this landmark offers a glimpse into a turbulent past, sparking conversations about the Marcos legacy.

02. Diocese of San Pablo Tour

The history of Cabuyao, Laguna's St. Polycarp Church is intricately linked to the growth of the municipality. It was once known as Tabuko, but the natives misinterpreted the Spanish question about the location for the name of a citrus tree, leading to the name Cabuyao. The first missionaries from Augustinians arrived in 1571, and then the Franciscans constructed the first church in a coastal region that was prone to flooding. On higher land within the town proper, a new church dedicated to St. Polycarp was built between 1763 and 1771. This church was the scene of important occasions. During the Japanese occupation, it functioned as a stable for horses and a haven for injured soldiers. In 1935, it also turned into the fatal site of a fight between Sakdalistas and the police.

03. University of Cabuyao Tour

In the Philippine province of Laguna, the city of Cabuyao is home to the Pamantasan ng Cabuyao (PnC), sometimes referred to as the University of Cabuyao. Proceso "Etok" D, the mayor at the time, founded it. Aguillo, via the adoption of the April 16, 2003, authorised Municipal Ordinance 2003-059. There was a satellite campus of Laguna State Polytechnic University (LSPU) in the Cabuyao National High School prior to the founding of the Pamantasan ng Cabuyao. The LSPU was run under a memorandum of understanding between the mayor of Cabuyao Municipality and the LSPU president, which ran from 1993 until March 31, 2003.

05. Garden Resort Tour

In Cabuyao, Laguna, Queen Aurora Garden Resort welcomes with a peaceful haven. This laid-back resort is perfect for people looking for a refuge inspired by nature. Mapúa

06. MMCL Tour

MMCL (previously MCL) is a private research university located in Laguna that was founded in 2006. It is owned by Mapúa University and provides courses in business, IT, engineering, and other subjects. Renowned for its impressive job placement rates, MMCL has expanded from its eight founding programmes to a wide array of academic offerings, all of which are ideally situated close to Laguna's industrial centre to give students access to worthwhile internship opportunities. The transition to MMCL in 2023 cemented the organization's affiliation with the esteemed Mapúa University. In keeping with this growth, MMCL Senior High School was established to serve students looking for a smooth transition from high school to college inside the MMCL system.

07. Science Park Tour

Situated 35 kilometres south of Makati, the nation's economic hub, and accessible via the South Luzon Motorway, is Light Industry & Science Park I, the first privately-owned industrial park to be registered with the Board of Investments. Situated on 178 hectares, it was fully booked in 1995. 92 locators call it home, including Fortune 500 business Procter & Gamble. Over 28,000 workers are employed thanks to LISP I locators.

08. Nestle Cabuyao Tour

The Cabuyao Factory is the Nestlé Export Centre for infant feeding products, and it is situated on a 25-hectare plot of land in Barangay Niugan, Cabuyao, Laguna. Having been founded in 1976, the company produces an extensive array of goods such as NAN, NESTOGEN, BEAR BRAND, NIDO, NESVITA Milks, NESTLÉ ALL-PURPOSE CREAM, Ready-to-Drink MILO, BEAR BRAND, and CHUCKIE. An essential part of Nestlé's international activities is the Cabuyao Factory. In addition to producing a lot of goods, it acts as a major hub for the export of baby food items. Nestlé's dedication to supplying the next generation with necessary sustenance is shown in their focus on newborn feeding. The extensive product line produced in Cabuyao serves a variety of age and dietary requirements, guaranteeing a consistent supply of reliable Nestlé brands for Filipino households as well as foreign markets.
